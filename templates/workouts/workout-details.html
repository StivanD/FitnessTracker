Beginner Strength Training

Category: Strength

This beginner strength training workout is designed to build your foundation. You'll be guided through key exercises to help you gain strength and confidence.

Workout Overview
  • Duration: 30 minutes
  • Difficulty: Beginner
  • Equipment Needed: Dumbbells, Resistance Bands
  • Calories Burned: ~200 kcal
Workout Breakdown

This workout is divided into three main phases: Warm-up, Strength Exercises, and Cool-down.

  1. Warm-up (5 minutes) - Light cardio and dynamic stretches to prepare your body for the workout.
  2. Strength Training (20 minutes) - A series of resistance exercises targeting the major muscle groups. Focus on form and controlled movements.
  3. Cool-down (5 minutes) - Static stretches to reduce muscle tightness and improve flexibility.
Detailed Exercise List
  • Squats - 3 sets of 12 reps. Strengthen the legs, glutes, and core.
  • Push-ups - 3 sets of 10 reps. Target the chest, shoulders, and triceps.
  • Plank - 3 sets of 30 seconds. Engage the core, shoulders, and glutes.
  • Dumbbell Rows - 3 sets of 12 reps. Focus on the back and biceps.
Instructor Tips

Form is Key: Make sure to keep your back straight during squats and rows. A slight bend in your knees during push-ups will reduce strain on your shoulders.

Common Mistakes: Avoid arching your back during planks or letting your knees cave in during squats.

Benefits of This Workout
  • Increased Strength: Build muscle and strength, especially in the lower body and upper body.
  • Improved Posture: Targeting the core and back muscles helps improve your posture.
  • Boosted Confidence: As you see progress, your strength and confidence will grow.
What to Expect

After completing this workout, you should feel a sense of accomplishment, a bit of muscle soreness (especially if you're new to strength training), and a boost in overall energy levels.
